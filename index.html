Researches during MSc, Amirkabir University of Technology, Tehran, Iran

Thesis

I analyzed and compared four different models including LIFT, Deep learning (two-layered and three-layered RBMs), and Graph-based models e.g Rank method and Label Propagation consider three different evaluation metrics which are appropriate to assess multi-label classification i.e Accuracy, Hamming Loss, and Average Precision.

Eventually, I was successful in improving the Label Propagation method which yielded better results in Accuracy and Hamming Loss for Genbase, Enron, and Image datasets. The Label Propagation method consists of two main phases:

  1. Preprocessing, and converting scalar data to graph data to shape a network, edges define similarity between nodes, in the following procedure, prunes edges with less pre-defined similarity threshold.
  2. Adding new unseen data as a new node to the network, and starting propagation from the node to all other nodes, and in the final state, all propagated nodes will be counted and assigned the winner label to the unseen node.

In the suggested approach, to improve results, after the first phase, some extra nodes will be added to the network to balance the whole network.

Algorithm vs Datasets Genbase Yeast Enron Image
LIFT 0.982 0.5194 0.3497 0.4356
Three-layered RBM 0.0089 0.4566 0.3072 0.3653
Propagation with extra nodes 0.9933 0.541 0.3269 0.4403

In a nutshell, it is considerably conspicuous that the LIFT technique had better results for the Average Precision metric for all datasets, but Propagation with extra nodes approach showed better results in Accuracy Criteria for all datasets except Enron, and better Hamming Loss for Genbase and Enron datasets.
